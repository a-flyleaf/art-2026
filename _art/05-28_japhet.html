A character design that has inexplicably stuck with me is Japhet [OFFgame](https://offtherpg.com/). Cat eats birb, birb puppets cat from within, birb bursts out of cat's mouth pixel-horror-style in the buildup to the boss fight. It's pretty cool! Anyway, this was a great modeling challenge. Did a big complicated rig All By Myself™! He's not T-posed by default but whatever!! Features a smidge of Grease Pencil for the eye & beak outlines, some crumb of [scenery](https://lparchive.org/OFF/Update%2012/), and one of Blender's default [HDRI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QNr7lwhW0h4)s for the lighting. My initial plan was for a `#ffffff` (all white) model, shadeless or [cel-only](https://youtu.be/tWcaQ3gCbUU?t=149), with physics on Valerie (the cat) to make him all limp. But I like shiny materials and, despite the tedium, to have full control over bone positioning. Main thing I learned with this model ended up being how bone groups and (some) constraints work. Did not use Rigify, but generated and poked around at its default models to get a better sense of how it actually works. As a side note, the shading is only super shiny and reflective-looking like this with Blender's Smooth shading turned all the way on. Compare the main render [above]({%include url.html%}/assets/art/japhet_12-smooth.png) to the auto-smoothed version: The wing bones could be tweaked further for ease of use, but overall I'm happy with how this turned out! Uses Blender 2.8 but still entirely workable, give or take the UI (user interface, menus buttons etc.). - StackExchange: [keeping IK feet flat](https://blender.stackexchange.com/questions/91802/keep-rig-feet-flat-on-the-ground-ik-rig). Now *that's* an old UI! - YT: [Rigging Eyes in 60 Seconds](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xSsav9wlDwI). Japhet's eyes don't move, but I used this for the head; it follows a single bone floating in front of the beak, carrot-on-a-stick style. - YT: [animating a tail (short)](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/GrH4HP5elvs) + [I poorly explain the damped track constraint](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SMZam1zFAz4). I considered using this for the wings, but ended up preferring [Copy Rotation](https://docs.blender.org/manual/en/latest/animation/constraints/transform/copy_rotation.html) for more control. - YT: [How to Join or Combine Armatures](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oj_2_1XkgNw). TL;DR join it, then re-parent the child's root bone(s) accordingly. - r/blenderHelp: [unifying bone colors](https://old.reddit.com/r/blenderhelp/comments/18zuybs/trouble_giving_bones_collection_unanimous_colour/l0twyu2/). `ALT` is key! - StackExchange: [parenting Grease Pencil to an armature](https://blender.stackexchange.com/questions/90915/how-to-parent-a-grease-pencil-with-armature).
